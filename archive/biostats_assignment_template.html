8.2 Risk in each group

\[ Risk_{exposed} = \frac{a}{a+b} \]

\[ Risk_{unexposed} = \frac{c}{c+d} \]

8.3 Risk ratio

\[ RR = \frac{a/(a+b)}{c/(c+d)} \]

Risk ratio: 4.000

8.4 Odds ratio

\[ OR = \frac{a \times d}{b \times c} \]

Odds ratio: 10.000

8.5 Optional: approximate 95% confidence interval for the odds ratio

\[ SE\left[\ln(OR)\right] = \sqrt{\frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{b} + \frac{1}{c} + \frac{1}{d}} \]

\[ 95\%\ CI = \exp\left(\ln(OR) \pm 1.96 \times SE\left[\ln(OR)\right]\right) \]

10.2 Confounding

Example:

12 Interpretation

Provide a concise interpretation of the main result.

Example:

The estimated risk ratio was greater than 1, suggesting that exposure may be associated with a higher risk of the outcome in this sample. The odds ratio was in the same direction. However, the findings should be interpreted cautiously because the sample is small and potential confounding has not yet been addressed.
