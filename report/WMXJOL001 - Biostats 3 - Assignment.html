Question 4

Present a single table (Table 2) with coefficient estimates, standard errors, and 95% confidence intervals (CI) for both GEE models. Use robust (sandwich) standard errors throughout

Caution

Table 2 presents the estimated associations between marital satisfaction and time and covariates from two GEE models with different working correlation structures.

There is evidence that marital satisfaction decreases over time, as indicated by the negative coefficient for time in the AR(1) model. This suggests that, on average, satisfaction declines with increasing follow-up time. However, the estimate from the unstructured model is less precise and includes the null, indicating some sensitivity to the choice of working correlation structure.

Sex is associated with satisfaction, with males reporting lower satisfaction than females in the AR(1) model. This effect is less consistent in the unstructured model, where the confidence interval includes zero.

Household workload is negatively associated with satisfaction, with higher workload associated with lower satisfaction in the AR(1) model. This effect is less precise in the unstructured model.

There is no strong evidence of association between satisfaction and age at marriage, premarital cohabitation, or income, as the confidence intervals for these variables include zero in both models.

Overall, the estimates are broadly similar across the two models, although some differences in precision and statistical significance are observed, suggesting that the results are reasonably robust but somewhat sensitive to the assumed correlation structure.

Question 5

Determine the most appropriate correlation structure and justify your choice. State which GEE (GEE 1 or GEE 2) you will use as your final model.

Caution

The AR(1) correlation structure was judged to be the most appropriate. It is conceptually suitable for longitudinal data because it assumes that correlations decrease as observations become further apart in time. Empirically, the AR(1) model produced more stable coefficient estimates and narrower confidence intervals than the unstructured model, whereas the unstructured model showed less precision and greater instability, likely due to the need to estimate many correlation parameters in an unbalanced dataset. Therefore, the AR(1) structure was preferred.

Time

Premarital cohabitation

Household workload

Question 8

Determine the most appropriate random-effects structure and justify your choice. State which LME (LME 1 or LME 2) is your final model.

LME 2 is a nested version of LME 1, adding random slopes for time. Fixed effect estimates are similar in both models, indicating consistency across models. However, LME 2 has higher log-likelihood and lower AIC and BIC values than LME 1, indicating a better fit to the data. The random slope variance in LME 2 is non-zero, suggesting meaningful between-individual variation in time trends that is not captured by random intercepts alone. This relates to the spagetti plot (Figure 2) which showed clear individual variation in trajectories over time, supporting the need for random slopes.
